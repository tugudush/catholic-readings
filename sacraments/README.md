# The Seven Sacraments of the Catholic Church

A comprehensive theological and historical study of the seven sacraments of the Roman Catholic Church, drawn from Sacred Scripture, the ecumenical councils, papal magisterial documents, the [_Catechism of the Catholic Church_](https://www.magisterium.com/docs/0583c069-d4bf-42dd-97de-c19f0b80150f/ref/para-1), the Code of Canon Law, and the Code of Canons of the Eastern Churches. Both the **Latin Rite** and the **Eastern Catholic _sui iuris_ Churches** are presented explicitly, in fidelity to the communion of 24 churches that constitutes the Catholic Church (see [church-history/eastern-catholic-churches.md](../church-history/eastern-catholic-churches.md)).

---

## 1. Foundational Theology of the Sacraments

### 1.1 Definition

The Catholic Church teaches that the **sacraments are efficacious signs of grace, instituted by Christ and entrusted to the Church, by which divine life is dispensed to us** ([CCC 1131](https://www.magisterium.com/docs/0583c069-d4bf-42dd-97de-c19f0b80150f/ref/1131)). The visible rites by which the sacraments are celebrated signify and make present the graces proper to each sacrament, and they bear fruit in those who receive them with the required dispositions.

This doctrine was solemnly defined by the **Council of Trent, Seventh Session (1547)**, which anathematized anyone who denies that the seven sacraments of the New Law were all instituted by Jesus Christ our Lord, or who denies that any one of them is truly and properly a sacrament ([Council of Trent, Session VII, Canon I](https://www.magisterium.com/docs/4a72892b-0a56-4371-a138-ff4aea2b0093/ref/)); Trent further defined that the sacraments _contain_ the grace they signify and _confer_ that grace on those who do not place an obstacle ([Council of Trent, Session VII, Canon VI](https://www.magisterium.com/docs/4a72892b-0a56-4371-a138-ff4aea2b0093/ref/)).

By the action of Christ and the power of the Holy Spirit, the sacraments _make present efficaciously_ the grace that they signify ([CCC 1084](https://www.magisterium.com/docs/0583c069-d4bf-42dd-97de-c19f0b80150f/ref/1084)). They are not merely outward symbols or pious rituals: Christ himself acts through them.

### 1.2 The Seven Sacraments

The Catholic Church professes exactly **seven sacraments**, as defined by the [Council of Trent](https://www.magisterium.com/docs/4a72892b-0a56-4371-a138-ff4aea2b0093/ref/) and the ecumenical Council of Florence in its _Decretum pro Armenis_ (1438–1445). The Catechism groups them as follows:

| Group | Sacraments |
| --- | --- |
| **Sacraments of Christian Initiation** | Baptism, Confirmation (Chrismation in the East), Eucharist |
| **Sacraments of Healing** | Penance and Reconciliation, Anointing of the Sick |
| **Sacraments at the Service of Communion and Mission** | Holy Orders, Matrimony |

- [Baptism](baptism.md) — the gateway to the spiritual life and incorporation into Christ and the Church.
- [Confirmation / Chrismation](confirmation.md) — the sealing of the Holy Spirit that completes baptismal grace.
- [The Most Holy Eucharist](eucharist.md) — the source and summit of the Christian life, the Body, Blood, Soul, and Divinity of Jesus Christ.
- [Penance and Reconciliation](penance-reconciliation.md) — the forgiveness of sins committed after Baptism.
- [Order of Confession](order-of-confession.md) — a practical step-by-step guide to making a confession, with examination of conscience and Eastern Catholic comparison.
- [Anointing of the Sick](anointing-of-the-sick.md) — strength, peace, and the forgiveness of sins for the seriously ill.
- [Holy Orders](holy-orders.md) — the apostolic ministry in three degrees: episcopate, presbyterate, diaconate.
- [Matrimony](matrimony.md) — the conjugal covenant raised by Christ to the dignity of a sacrament.

#### Related Canonical Documents

- [Apostasy & Formal Defection](apostasy-formal-defection.md) — the dogma of the indelible baptismal character, the canonical institute of formal defection and its abrogation by Benedict XVI's _Omnium in Communione_ (2009), the present juridical status of Catholics who join other religions, and the sacramental and marriage-law implications.

### 1.3 Matter, Form, and Minister

Catholic sacramental theology classically distinguishes the **matter** (the visible element or action), the **form** (the essential words pronounced), and the **minister** (the one who validly performs the rite). The reception of the sacrament requires, on the part of the believer, the proper **intention** and **dispositions** (faith, repentance, charity, and the willingness to receive what the sacrament signifies).

### 1.4 The Sacramental Character

Three sacraments — **Baptism, Confirmation, and Holy Orders** — imprint an **indelible character** (a permanent spiritual seal) on the soul. For this reason, they can never be repeated ([CCC 1597](https://www.magisterium.com/docs/0583c069-d4bf-42dd-97de-c19f0b80150f/ref/1597); Council of Florence, _Exsultate Deo_, 1439). The character configures the Christian permanently to Christ and his Church, and it is a mark of belonging that nothing can erase.

### 1.5 Sacred Scripture and the Seven Sacraments

The seven sacraments are rooted in the mysteries of Christ's life and in his direct actions reported in the New Testament:

- **Baptism** is willed by Christ in [Matthew 28:19](https://www.magisterium.com/docs/00a1de35-98f1-481a-956f-e98e2b52331d/ref/) and [John 3:5](https://www.magisterium.com/docs/00a1de35-98f1-481a-956f-e98e2b52331d/ref/).
- **Confirmation** is grounded in the apostolic _laying on of hands_ in [Acts 8:14–17](https://www.magisterium.com/docs/00a1de35-98f1-481a-956f-e98e2b52331d/ref/) and [Acts 19:5–6](https://www.magisterium.com/docs/00a1de35-98f1-481a-956f-e98e2b52331d/ref/).
- **The Eucharist** is instituted at the Last Supper in [Matthew 26:26–29](https://www.magisterium.com/docs/00a1de35-98f1-481a-956f-e98e2b52331d/ref/), [Mark 14:22–25](https://www.magisterium.com/docs/00a1de35-98f1-481a-956f-e98e2b52331d/ref/), [Luke 22:14–20](https://www.magisterium.com/docs/00a1de35-98f1-481a-956f-e98e2b52331d/ref/), and [1 Corinthians 11:23–26](https://www.magisterium.com/docs/00a1de35-98f1-481a-956f-e98e2b52331d/ref/).
- **Penance** is instituted by Christ in [John 20:22–23](https://www.magisterium.com/docs/00a1de35-98f1-481a-956f-e98e2b52331d/ref/) ("Receive the Holy Spirit. Whose sins you forgive are forgiven them").
- **Anointing of the Sick** is grounded in [James 5:14–15](https://www.magisterium.com/docs/00a1de35-98f1-481a-956f-e98e2b52331d/ref/) and alluded to in [Mark 6:13](https://www.magisterium.com/docs/00a1de35-98f1-481a-956f-e98e2b52331d/ref/).
- **Holy Orders** is grounded in the apostolic ministry transmitted by the _laying on of hands_ ([1 Timothy 4:14](https://www.magisterium.com/docs/00a1de35-98f1-481a-956f-e98e2b52331d/ref/), [2 Timothy 1:6](https://www.magisterium.com/docs/00a1de35-98f1-481a-956f-e98e2b52331d/ref/), [Titus 1:5](https://www.magisterium.com/docs/00a1de35-98f1-481a-956f-e98e2b52331d/ref/)).
- **Matrimony** is rooted in the creation of man and woman in [Genesis 1:27–28](https://www.magisterium.com/docs/00a1de35-98f1-481a-956f-e98e2b52331d/ref/), restored and elevated by Christ in [Matthew 19:3–9](https://www.magisterium.com/docs/00a1de35-98f1-481a-956f-e98e2b52331d/ref/), and revealed as a great mystery in [Ephesians 5:21–33](https://www.magisterium.com/docs/00a1de35-98f1-481a-956f-e98e2b52331d/ref/).

## 2. Latin Rite and Eastern Catholic Rites

The Catholic Church is a communion of **24 churches** — the Latin Church and **23 Eastern Catholic _sui iuris_ churches** — that share the same faith, the same seven sacraments, and the same profession of the Creed, but that retain distinct liturgical rites, theological emphases, and disciplinary traditions ([church-history/eastern-catholic-churches.md](../church-history/eastern-catholic-churches.md)). Wherever a sacrament differs between East and West — in minister, in the exact matter or form, in discipline, or in liturgical emphasis — the present documentation presents both traditions explicitly.

Notable examples include:

- **Baptism**: in the Latin Rite, ordinarily conferred in infancy; in the Eastern Churches, often celebrated with Chrismation and first Communion in infancy in a single initiation rite.
- **Confirmation / Chrismation**: in the Latin Rite, ordinarily reserved to the bishop; in the Eastern Catholic Churches, ordinarily conferred by the priest immediately after Baptism.
- **Holy Orders**: in the Latin Rite, only celibate men are ordained to the priesthood; in the Eastern Catholic Churches, married men may be ordained to the diaconate and presbyterate, while bishops are chosen solely from among celibate men.
- **Matrimony**: in the Latin Rite, the spouses are the ministers; in many Eastern liturgies, the crowning by the priest or bishop constitutes the visible liturgical sign after the spouses' consent.

Theological content, however, is not in dispute between East and West: the same seven sacraments, the same essential matter and form, the same doctrinal effects, and the same salvation-historical meaning are professed everywhere in the Catholic communion.

## 3. Levels of Authority in This Documentation

Each sacrament file in this directory follows the doctrinal conventions of the repository (see [AGENTS.md](../AGENTS.md)):

1. **Dogma** — what is divinely revealed and defined by an ecumenical council or the ordinary and universal Magisterium (e.g., the sevenfold number of the sacraments, the Real Presence of Christ in the Eucharist, transubstantiation).
2. **Discipline** — mutable pastoral norms that differ between East and West (e.g., the Latin Rite discipline of clerical celibacy, the age of Confirmation, the minister of Chrismation).
3. **Theological opinion** — scholastic or pastoral explanations that help illuminate doctrine but are not of faith (e.g., the precise philosophical elaboration of transubstantiation).
4. **Private revelation** — never invoked as a doctrinal source for the sacraments themselves, which rest entirely on public Revelation and apostolic tradition.
