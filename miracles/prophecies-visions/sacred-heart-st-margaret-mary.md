# The Sacred Heart of Jesus and Saint Margaret Mary Alacoque (1673-1675)

## Theological Introduction
The devotions and revelations connected to the Sacred Heart of Jesus stand as one of the most powerful and magisterially endorsed private revelations in Church history:
- While private revelations do not alter the deposit of faith (CCC 67), the devotion to the Sacred Heart reflects a deep biblical truth: God's love is not abstract but has been made physical and visible in the human Heart of the Incarnate Word.
- The Church has repeatedly elevated and defended this devotion through numerous papal encyclicals, notably Pope Pius XII's *Haurietis Aquas* (1956) and Pope Leo XIII's *Annum Sacrum* (1899).

## The Revelations at Paray-le-Monial
Between 1673 and 1675, Saint Margaret Mary Alacoque, a sister of the Visitation Order in Paray-le-Monial, France, received four major apparitions of Jesus Christ.

### Key Historical and Mystical Details:
1. **The First Apparition (December 27, 1673)**: While praying before the Blessed Sacrament, Jesus appeared to her, allowing her to rest her head upon his chest. He showed her his Heart burning with flames, surrounded by a crown of thorns, and surmounted by a cross. He explained that his Heart was overflowing with a passionate love for humanity and that he wished to manifest this love to the world through her.
2. **The Call for Reparation**: Jesus expressed deep sorrow over the ingratitude, coldness, and indifference that humanity returned for his love, particularly in their neglect and irreverence toward the Sacrament of the Holy Eucharist.
3. **Core Devotional Requests**:
   - **Frequent Communion**: Especially receiving Holy Communion on the **First Friday** of each month for nine consecutive months (known as the Nine First Fridays).
   - **The Holy Hour**: Keeping an hour of prayer in reparation every Thursday night from 11:00 p.m. to midnight, joining in Christ's agony in the Garden of Gethsemane.
   - **The Feast of the Sacred Heart**: Requesting the establishment of a liturgical feast dedicated to his Sacred Heart on the Friday following the Octave of Corpus Christi.
4. **The Twin Pillars of Support**: Saint Margaret Mary found immense skepticism within her community. However, Christ provided her a spiritual director, **Saint Claude de la Colombière, SJ**, a holy Jesuit priest who recognized the authenticity of her mystical experiences and helped her document and spread the devotion.

## The Twelve Promises of the Sacred Heart
In her letters, Saint Margaret Mary recorded twelve promises that Jesus made to those who honor his Sacred Heart, which include:
1. I will give them all the graces necessary for their state of life.
2. I will establish peace in their families.
3. I will comfort them in all their afflictions.
4. I will be their secure refuge during life, and above all, in death.
5. I will bestow abundant blessings upon all their undertakings.
6. Sinners shall find in my Heart the source and infinite ocean of mercy.
7. Lukewarm souls shall become fervent.
8. Fervent souls shall quickly mount to high perfection.
9. I will bless every place in which an image of my Heart is exposed and honored.
10. I will give to priests the gift of touching the most hardened hearts.
11. Those who shall promote this devotion shall have their names written in my Heart.
12. **The Great Promise**: I promise you in the excessive mercy of my Heart that my all-powerful love will grant to all those who receive Holy Communion on the First Fridays in nine consecutive months the grace of final perseverance; they shall not die in my disfavor of receiving their sacraments.

## Ecclesiastical Approvals and Universal Feast
- **Liturgical Feast**: In 1765, Pope Clement XIII officially approved the celebration of the Feast of the Sacred Heart for Poland and the Archconfraternity of Rome. In 1856, Pope Pius IX extended the Feast of the Sacred Heart to the universal Church.
- **Consecration of the World**: On June 11, 1899, Pope Leo XIII solemnly consecrated the entire human race to the Sacred Heart of Jesus (*Annum Sacrum*), declaring it the greatest act of his pontificate.
- **Canonization**: Saint Margaret Mary Alacoque was beatified by Pope Pius IX in 1864 and solemnly canonized by Pope Benedict XV on May 13, 1920. Her spiritual director, Claude de la Colombière, was canonized by Pope John Paul II in 1992.
