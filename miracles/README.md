# Catholic Miracles, Apparitions, Visions, and Prophecies

Welcome to the central directory documenting approved supernatural phenomena, wonders, and private revelations within the Catholic Church. 

The Catholic Church maintains a posture of careful, scientifically rigorous, and pastoral discernment toward alleged supernatural occurrences, distinguishing clearly between the definitive public Revelation of Jesus Christ and private revelations.

---

## 1. Marian Apparitions
Marian apparitions are occurrences where the Blessed Virgin Mary is understood to have physically appeared to one or more seers with messages of prayer, repentance, and conversion.

* **[Our Lady of Guadalupe (1531)](marian-apparitions/guadalupe.md)**: The appearance of Our Lady to Saint Juan Diego in Mexico in December 1531, which produced the miraculous tilma image and catalyzed the evangelization of the Americas.
* **[Our Lady of Lourdes (1858)](marian-apparitions/lourdes.md)**: The 18 apparitions of Mary as the "Immaculate Conception" to Saint Bernadette Soubirous in France, and the uncovering of the miraculous grotto spring.
* **[Our Lady of Fatima (1917)](marian-apparitions/fatima.md)**: The apparitions to Francisco, Jacinta, and Lúcia in Portugal, featuring the Three Secrets and the Miracle of the Sun.
* **[Our Lady of La Salette (1846)](marian-apparitions/la-salette.md)**: The sorrowful, weeping appearance of Mary to Mélanie Calvat and Maximin Giraud on a French mountain.
* **[The Miraculous Medal (1830)](marian-apparitions/miraculous-medal.md)**: The revelations to Saint Catherine Labouré in Paris, directing the striking of the famous medal bearing sacramental graces.
* **[Our Lady of Knock (1879)](marian-apparitions/knock.md)**: The silent, liturgical, Eucharistic apparition in Ireland, depicting Mary, Saint Joseph, Saint John the Evangelist, and the Lamb on the Altar.
* **[Our Lady of Akita (1973)](marian-apparitions/akita.md)**: The modern apparitions and miraculous lacrimations of a wooden statue witnessed by Sister Agnes Sasagawa in Japan.

## 2. Eucharistic Miracles
Eucharistic miracles are physical signs that confirm the dogma of the Real Presence of Christ and the conversion of Transubstantiation, where the consecrated species visibly take on the attributes of human flesh and blood.

* **[The Eucharistic Miracle of Bolsena-Orvieto (1263)](eucharistic-miracles/bolsena-orvieto.md)**: The bleeding Host that stained the corporal of Father Peter of Prague, prompting Pope Urban IV to institute the universal Feast of Corpus Christi.
* **[The Eucharistic Miracle of Lanciano (c. 750 AD)](eucharistic-miracles/lanciano.md)**: The earliest scientifically analyzed miracle, where the Host and wine became human cardiac muscle tissue (AB blood type) and remain perfectly preserved today.

## 3. Miracles and Wonders of the Saints
Phenomena and supernatural signs surrounding the earthly lives and relics of the canonized saints.

* **[The Stigmata of Saint Pio of Pietrelcina](saint-phenomena/stigmata-padre-pio.md)**: The physical configuration of Padre Pio to Christ's Passion, bearing his bleeding wounds for exactly fifty years until his death.
* **[The Liquefaction of the Blood of Saint Januarius](saint-phenomena/liquefaction-st-januarius.md)**: The century-spanning prodigy of Milan/Naples where the ancient, dried blood of the martyr liquefies on specific feast days.

## 4. Prophecies and Heavenly Visions
Mystical revelations, visions of Christ, and prophetic directions entrusted to saints to guide and renew the Church.

* **[Divine Mercy and Saint Faustina](prophecies-visions/divine-mercy-st-faustina.md)**: The revelations, image, chaplet, and feast of Mercy revealed to Saint Maria Faustina Kowalska in 1930s Poland.
* **[The Sacred Heart and Saint Margaret Mary Alacoque](prophecies-visions/sacred-heart-st-margaret-mary.md)**: The apparitions of Christ's burning Heart to a Visitation nun in Paray-le-Monial, establishing the Holy Hour, First Fridays devotion, and the Feast of the Sacred Heart.

## Theological Principles of Discernment
1. **Definitive Public Revelation**: Catholic doctrine asserts that public Revelation ended with the death of the last Apostle. No private revelation can add to, subtract from, or correct this deposit of faith (CCC 66).
2. **Criteria for Approval**: The Dicastery for the Doctrine of the Faith (DDF) and bishops evaluate messages based on three pillars:
   * **Doctrinal Alignment**: The messages must contain nothing contrary to faith and morals.
   * **Seer Authenticity**: The seers must demonstrate psychological balance, humility, integrity, and obedience to the Church.
   * **Spiritual Fruits**: The event must lead to tangible spiritual growth, conversions, prayer, and charity.
3. **Levels of Assent**: Belief in approved apparitions is not a matter of dogmatic faith. Rather, the Church grants a *Nihil obstat* or episcopal declare stating the phenomenon is "worthy of belief," offering it to the faithful for their prudent spiritual benefit.
