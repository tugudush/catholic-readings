# The Eucharistic Miracle of Lanciano (c. 750 AD)

## Theological Introduction
The Eucharistic Miracle of Lanciano is the earliest and most famous of the recorded Eucharistic miracles in the Catholic Church, demonstrating:
- The distinction between substance and accidents: in this miracle, after the consecration, both the substance *and* the physical accidents of bread and wine were transformed into the visible appearance of human heart tissue and human blood.
- It serves as a powerful "motive of credibility" (CCC 156), providing empirical confirmation that the supernatural conversion of transubstantiation occurs at every Mass.

## The Apparition and Event
Around the year 750 AD, in the Church of Saints Legontian and Domitian in Lanciano, Italy, a priest of the Order of Saint Basil was celebrating the Holy Sacrifice of the Mass.

### Key Historical Details:
1. **The Priest's Doubt**: The priest was highly educated but suffered from a severe temptation and doubt regarding the Real Presence of Jesus Christ in the Eucharist, wondering if the bread and wine truly became the Body and Blood of Christ.
2. **The Transformation**: At the moment of consecration, as the priest pronounced the words over the host and the chalice, a physical transformation occurred in full view of his eyes:
   - The **Host** became a circle of living flesh.
   - The **wine** became living, dark red blood, which coagulated into five uniquely shaped globules.
3. **The Proclamation**: Overcome with awe and remorse, the trembling priest turned to the congregation and proclaimed:
   > "O fortunate witnesses, to whom the Blessed God, to confound my disbelief, has wished to reveal Himself in this Most Holy Sacrament and to render Himself visible to our eyes!"

## Modern Scientific Investigations
Unlike many ancient relics, the Lanciano relics have been subjected to rigorous, modern scientific testing, most notably between 1970 and 1971:
- **The Linoli Study**: The Archbishop of Lanciano and the Holy See authorized a scientific investigation under the direction of **Dr. Odoardo Linoli**, a professor of anatomy, pathological histology, chemistry, and clinical microscopy at the University of Siena.
- **Key Scientific Findings**:
  1. **The Flesh**: The flesh was determined to be genuine human **cardiac muscle tissue** (myocardium, specifically from the left ventricle). It includes the endocardium, vagus nerve, and-fibrous structures of the heart.
  2. **The Blood**: The blood was confirmed to be genuine **human blood**, classified as type **AB** (the same blood type consistently found on the Shroud of Turin and the Sudarium of Oviedo).
  3. **Preservation**: Despite being kept in poor environmental conditions in an unsealed reliquary for over 1,200 years of exposure to air and microorganisms, the relics contain no trace of artificial chemical preservatives, and the blood proteins were found to be intact, behaving exactly like living human blood.
  4. **The Five Globules**: The five blood clots, when weighed individually, have the bizarre property that any single globule weighs the exact same as all five weighed together, and any combination of them weighs the same as the total.

## Ecclesiastical Status
The miracle has been recognized by the local diocese and various Popes down through the centuries (including Boniface VIII, Clement VIII, and Leo XIII). The church is currently cared for by the Conventual Franciscan Friars. The relics of the miraculous flesh and blood remain visible to pilgrims today inside a silver and crystal monstrance in the Sanctuary of the Eucharistic Miracle in Lanciano, Abruzzo, Italy.
