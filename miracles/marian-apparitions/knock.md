# Our Lady of Knock (1879)

## Theological Introduction
The apparition at Knock is unique in the history of Catholic **private revelations** due to its silent, liturgical, and Eucharistic character:
- Private revelations serve as pastoral aids to help believers live the Gospel of Jesus Christ in concrete times and places (CCC 67).
- Unlike most other approved apparitions, the figures at Knock spoke no words. Instead, the apparition was a silent, visual sermon deeply tied to the mystery of the Mass, the Liturgy, and the Communion of Saints.

## The Apparition at Knock
On the evening of August 21, 1879, under a drenching rain, fifteen villagers ranging in age from 6 to 75 witnessed a silent, luminous apparition on the south gable wall of the parish church of Saint John the Baptist in Knock, County Mayo, Ireland.

### Key Historical Details:
1. **The Figures**: The witnesses saw three distinct, life-sized figures standing near the wall:
   - **The Blessed Virgin Mary**: Standing erect, eyes raised to heaven, wearing a white cloak and a brilliant gold crown. Her hands were raised to the shoulders in a posture of continuous prayer and intercession.
   - **Saint Joseph**: Standing to Mary's right, head bowed slightly toward her in respect.
   - **Saint John the Evangelist**: Standing to Mary's left, dressed as a bishop with a small miter, holding an open book in his left hand, and raising his right hand as if preaching.
2. **The Eucharistic Altar**: To the left of Saint John, there was a plain altar. Upon it stood:
   - A **Lamb** (symbolizing Christ, the Lamb of God who takes away the sin of the world).
   - A **Cross** standing behind the Lamb.
   - Hovering **Angels** surrounding the altar with fluttering wings.
3. **No Words Spoken**: No messages, warnings, or secrets were spoken. The figures remained completely silent throughout the two-hour apparition.
4. **The Rain Miracle**: Although a driving wind and rain-storm beat down on the village, the ground beneath the gable and the wall itself remained completely dry during the entire evening.

## Canonical Investigation and Official Recognition
- **The First Commission (1879)**: Within months of the event, Archbishop John MacHale of Tuam established a commission of inquiry. Over fifteen witnesses were interviewed. The commission ruled the testimonies to be "trustworthy and satisfactory."
- **The Second Commission (1936)**: Archbishop Gilmartin of Tuam opened a second commission of inquiry in 1936 to re-evaluate the surviving witnesses (including Mary Byrne, who gave her testimony on her deathbed). The commission re-confirmed the integrity and authenticity of their reports.
- **Papal Visits and Elevations**:
  - **Saint John Paul II**: Visited the shrine on September 30, 1979, to mark the centenary of the apparition, presenting a golden rose to the shrine and raising the church to the status of a minor basilica.
  - **Pope Francis**: Visited the shrine in August 2018 during his visit to Ireland. On March 19, 2021, Pope Francis officially elevated Knock Shrine to the status of an **International Marian and Eucharistic Sanctuary**, recognizing its theological and pastoral depth.
