# Our Lady of Fatima (1917)

## Theological Introduction to Private Revelation
The events at Fatima are considered by the Catholic Church to be among the most influential and prophetically significant **private revelations** of modern history:
- Private revelation must always be understood in subordination to the definitive public Revelation of Christ (Catechism of the Catholic Church, 67).
- In June 2000, the Congregation for the Doctrine of the Faith (CDF) issued a comprehensive document *The Message of Fatima*, which provided the authentic texts of the secrets of Fatima along with a detailed theological commentary by Cardinal Joseph Ratzinger (later Pope Benedict XVI).
- The CDF clarified that the Fatima message is a call to Christian conversion, prayer, penance, and trust in God's providence, directing the faithful back to the heart of the Gospel.

## The Apparitions to the Three Shepherds
Between May 13 and October 13, 1917, the Blessed Virgin Mary appeared six times to three young shepherd children—Lúcia dos Santos (age 10) and her cousins Francisco (age 9) and Jacinta Marto (age 7)—in the Cova da Iria near Fatima, Portugal.

### Key Historical Details:
1. **The Core Request**: Mary requested daily recitation of the Holy Rosary for peace in the world, the offering of sacrifices in reparation for sins, and individual conversion.
2. **The Three Parts of the Secret**: During the third apparition on July 13, 1917, Mary confided a secret in three parts:
   - **The First Part**: A terrifying vision of hell, showing where those who reject God's love are headed, urging prayers for the conversion of sinners.
   - **The Second Part**: An instruction to establish throughout the world devotion to the Immaculate Heart of Mary. It warned of a second, worse war (World War II) if humanity did not cease offending God, requested the consecration of Russia to her Immaculate Heart, and foretold that Russia would "spread her errors throughout the world" (resulting in totalitarian atheism and persecutions of the Church).
   - **The Third Part**: A highly symbolic, apocalyptic vision of a "Bishop dressed in White" climbing a rugged mountain towards a rough-hewn cross, passing through a half-ruined city filled with corpses, who is then killed by soldiers. The Church interprets this as representing the sufferings of the 20th-century popes and martyrs, particularly reflected in the assassination attempt on Saint John Paul II on May 13, 1981 (the feast of Our Lady of Fatima).
3. **The Miracle of the Sun (October 13, 1917)**: On the day of Mary's final appearance, after months of skepticism and government hostility, an estimated 70,000 to 100,000 people gathered in heavy rain. Suddenly, the clouds parted, and the sun appeared as a spinning disc that whirled, cast multi-colored lights across the landscape, and seemed to plunge toward the earth. The phenomenon was witnessed miles away by skeptics and scientists alike. When it ended, the mud-soaked ground and the wet clothes of the crowd were completely dry.

## Papal Consecrations and Actions
Multiple popes have recognized and participated in the mysteries of Fatima:
- **Pope Pius XII**: Consecrated the world (1942) and specifically Russia (1952) to the Immaculate Heart of Mary, and established the liturgical feast.
- **Saint John Paul II**: Intensely devoted to Our Lady of Fatima after surviving the 1981 shooting, he traveled to Fatima to thank Mary, placing the bullet that struck him in her crown. On March 25, 1984, in spiritual union with the world's bishops, he solemnly consecrated Russia and the world to the Immaculate Heart, which Sister Lúcia personally confirmed fulfilled Our Lady's request.
- **The CDF Document (2000)**: Under John Paul II, the CDF fully published the handwritten text of the Third Secret, confirming its authenticity.

## Ecclesiastical Approval and Canonization
- **Canonical Approval**: On October 13, 1930, Bishop José Alves Correia da Silva of Leiria declared the apparitions "worthy of belief" and authorized public devotion.
- **Canonization of the Seers**: Francisco and Jacinta Marto died young, as Mary had predicted, in the influenza pandemic of 1919–1920. Both were beatified in 2000 and canonized by Pope Francis in Fatima on May 13, 2017. Lúcia dos Santos entered the Discalced Carmelite Order and died in 2005 at the age of 97; her cause for beatification is currently underway, and she has been declared Venerable.
