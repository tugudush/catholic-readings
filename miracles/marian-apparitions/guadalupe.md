# Our Lady of Guadalupe (1531)

## Theological Introduction to Private Revelation
The appearances of Our Lady of Guadalupe fall under the theological category of **private revelation**. According to Catholic doctrine:
- Private revelations do not belong to the deposit of faith. Their role is not to complete or improve Christ's definitive Revelation, but to help the faithful live more fully by it in a certain period of history (Catechism of the Catholic Church, 67).
- A private revelation does not demand theological faith (assent due to the Gospel), but rather a "prudent adhesion" when approved by Church authority.
- The principal criterion for judging the truth of any private revelation is its orientation to Christ—it must lead the faithful back to the definitive public Revelation of the Gospel (Pope Benedict XVI, Verbum Domini, 14).

## The Apparitions at Tepeyac
In December 1531, on the hill of Tepeyac (near modern-day Mexico City), the Blessed Virgin Mary appeared to Saint Juan Diego Cuauhtlatoatzin, a simple indigenous convert.

### Key Historical Details:
1. **First Encounters**: Mary appeared in native Nahua attire, speaking in Juan Diego's mother tongue (Nahuatl). She identified herself as the "ever perfect Holy Virgin Mary, Mother of the true God."
2. **The Request**: She requested that a church be built on Tepeyac hill where she could show her motherly love, compassion, and protection to all who sought her help.
3. **The Bishop's Skepticism**: Juan Diego relayed this request to the first bishop of Mexico, Fray Juan de Zumárraga, who was skeptical and asked for a miraculous sign to authenticate the heavenly messenger.
4. **Mary's Comforting Words**: When Juan Diego was filled with distress and concern over his sick uncle (Juan Bernardino), Mary comforted him with words preserved in the Nahuatl account, the *Nican Mopohua*:
   > "Let nothing frighten you, let nothing trouble your heart... Am I not here, I who am your mother? Are you not under my shadow and protection?"
5. **The Miracle of the Roses**: Mary directed Juan Diego to climb the dry, rocky peak of Tepeyac hill. Despite the freezing winter, he found beautiful Castilian roses growing there. He gathered them in his cloak (*tilma*).
6. **The Miraculous Image**: On December 12, 1531, when Juan Diego unfolded his tilma before Bishop Zumárraga to present the roses, the flowers fell to the floor, and a miraculous, highly symbolic image of Our Lady was found perfectly impressed upon the coarse cactus-fiber fabric of the tilma.

## Ecclesiastical Approval and Authority
The Guadalupan apparition is fully received, celebrated, and approved by the highest authorities of the Catholic Church:
- **Saint Juan Diego's Cause**: On April 9, 1990, a decree of the Congregation for the Causes of Saints recognizing his heroic virtues and immemorial cult was promulgated.
- **Beatification and Canonization**: Pope John Paul II beatified Juan Diego on May 6, 1990, and solemnly canonized him on July 31, 2002, in Mexico City, confirming the historic reality of the Tepeyac appearances.
- **Liturgical Recognition**: The feast of Our Lady of Guadalupe is celebrated as a solemnity in Mexico, a liturgical feast across the Americas on December 12, and is inscribed in the Roman Calendar. It is recognized as a supreme model of "perfectly inculturated evangelization."
