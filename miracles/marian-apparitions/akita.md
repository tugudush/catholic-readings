# Our Lady of Akita (1973)

## Theological Introduction
The manifestations connected to Our Lady of Akita represent a significant modern **private revelation**:
- As with all approved private revelations (CCC 67), its role is to help the faithful live the Gospel of Christ in a specific historical context, in this case, the late 20th century.
- The messages of Akita emphasize prayer, reparation, and the recitation of the Rosary, closely echoing the messages of Fatima.

## The Apparitions and Mystical Signs
Between 1973 and 1981, Sister Agnes Sasagawa, a religious sister of the Institute of the Handmaids of the Sacred Heart of Jesus in the Holy Eucharist in Akita, Japan, experienced several supernatural phenomena.

### Key Historical Details:
1. **The Statue of Mary**: Sister Agnes, who was deaf at the time, witnessed a wooden statue of the Virgin Mary weeping, sweating, and emitting blood. The statue, carved from a single piece of katsura wood, exhibited these phenomena on multiple occasions.
2. **The Lacrimations**: The wooden statue wept on 101 separate occasions between September 1975 and September 1981. This was witnessed by hundreds of people, including non-Catholics and the local Bishop, John Shojiro Ito.
3. **The Messages**: Sister Agnes received three messages from Our Lady through a voice from the statue. The messages warned of severe trials for the world and the Church if humanity did not repent and turn to God. She called for the daily recitation of the Rosary, prayer in reparation for the world's sins, and deep trust in divine providence.
4. **Sister Agnes's Healing**: Sister Agnes also received the stigmata (a painful wound on her hand) and was temporarily healed of her deafness, which later returned and was cured permanently on May 30, 1982, as foretold by the Virgin.

## Ecclesiastical Investigation and Approval
- **Scientific Analysis**: The fluids (tears, sweat, and blood) emitted by the wooden statue were scientifically analyzed by the forensic department of Akita University. The chemical tests confirmed that the substances were human bodily fluids, specifically of blood types O, B, and AB.
- **Episcopal Declaration (1984)**: On April 22, 1984, Bishop John Shojiro Ito of the Diocese of Niigata, after consulting with the Holy See, issued a pastoral letter declaring the events of Akita to be of supernatural origin and authorized the veneration of the Holy Mother of Akita within his diocese.
- **Holy See Status**: In June 1988, Bishop Ito met with Cardinal Joseph Ratzinger, then Prefect of the Congregation for the Doctrine of the Faith, who confirmed that the Bishop's pastoral letter was reliable and acceptable.
