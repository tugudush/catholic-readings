# The Stigmata of Saint Pio of Pietrelcina (Padre Pio)

## Theological Introduction
In Catholic theology, the **stigmata** is an extraordinary mystical phenomenon wherein a believer is physically configured to the suffering of Jesus Christ by bearing his Passion wounds (hands, feet, side, and head):
- The stigmata is viewed by the Church as a charism—a free spiritual gift given for the building up of the Church, rather than a necessary indicator of holiness in itself.
- Saint Paul writes in Galatians 6:17: "I bear on my body the marks of Jesus," which has historically been understood as a spiritual or physical configuration.
- The Church is extremely cautious in investigating alleged stigmata, conducting intensive medical and psychological evaluations to rule out psychological suggestion, self-harm, or fraud.

## The Mystical Wounds of Padre Pio
Francesco Forgione (who became the Capuchin friar Father Pio of Pietrelcina) is the most extensively documented and scientifically examined stigmatic priest in Church history.

### Key Historical Details:
1. **The Transverberation (August 20, 1918)**: While hearing confessions, Padre Pio experienced a mystical piercing of his soul (transverberation) by a celestial being holding a flaming lance, leaving him with a deep, physical wound in his side.
2. **The Stigmata (September 20, 1918)**: While praying before a crucifix in the choir loft of the Church of Santa Maria delle Grazie in San Giovanni Rotondo, Padre Pio received the visible stigmata. Wounds corresponding to the five wounds of Christ opened in his hands, feet, and side.
3. **Physical and Medical Characteristics**:
   - For fifty years, the wounds bled consistently (about a cup of blood daily), causing him intense pain.
   - The blood was noted to emit a sweet, floral fragrance (known as the "odour of sanctity") rather than a metallic iron smell.
   - Despite multiple rigorous examinations by diverse secular and Catholic physicians (including Dr. Luigi Romanelli, Dr. Giorgio Festa, and Dr. Amico Bignami), no natural medical explanation could be found. The wounds showed no signs of infection, suppuration, or natural healing, and remained stable for decades.
4. **The Complete Healing**: Shortly before his death on September 23, 1968, the wounds began to fade. At his final Mass, they were still visible, but immediately after his death, when his body was prepared, the wounds had evaporated completely without leaving a single scar or coloration, a phenomenon witnessed and photographed by doctors and friars.

## Canonical Investigation and Verification
- **Vatican Restrictions (1922-1933)**: Because of the massive, frenzied popular interest and complaints, the Holy Office investigated Padre Pio and temporarily restricted his public life. He was forbidden from celebrating Mass publicly, preaching, or answering letters, and his spiritual directors were replaced. Padre Pio submitted to these severe trials with complete, heroic obedience.
- **Lifting of Restrictions**: In 1933, Pope Pius XI began to lift the restrictions, declaring, "I have not been badly disposed toward Padre Pio, but I have been badly informed." By 1934, he was allowed to hear confessions and preach again.
- **Canonization**: Pope John Paul II, who had personally traveled to San Giovanni Rotondo to confess to Padre Pio in 1948, beatified him on May 2, 1999, and solemnly canonized him on June 16, 2002, proposing his life of prayer, confession, and conformity to Christ's Passion as an exemplar for the universal Church.
