# St. Cecilia: Virgin and Martyr, Patron of Musicians

## 1. Introduction

**St. Cecilia** (Latin: *Cæcilia*; c. 200 – c. 230 A.D.) was a Roman virgin martyr who is venerated as the **patron saint of musicians, sacred music, and the blind**. Her association with music arose from the tradition that she sang to God in her heart during her wedding feast. She is one of the most revered early Roman martyrs, mentioned in the Roman Canon of the Mass. Her feast day is **22 November**.

> "Make music in your hearts to the Lord."
> — St. Cecilia, echoing Ephesians 5:19

---

## 2. Life and Martyrdom

### Marriage and Vow of Virginity

According to the traditional account (recorded in the *Acts of St. Cecilia*, 5th c.), Cecilia was a young Roman noblewoman who had secretly **consecrated her virginity to God** through a vow. She was betrothed against her will to a young pagan nobleman named **Valerian**. On her wedding night, she told Valerian that an angel guarded her body and that he would see the angel if he were baptized.

### Conversion of Valerian and Tiburtius

Valerian, moved by her words, sought out **Pope Urban I** and was baptized. On returning to Cecilia, he saw an angel standing beside her, holding two crowns of roses and lilies. Valerian's brother **Tiburtius** was also converted and baptized.

### Martyrdom

The three—Cecilia, Valerian, and Tiburtius—were arrested under the persecution of Emperor **Alexander Severus** (or, in some accounts, Marcus Aurelius). Valerian and Tiburtius were beheaded. Cecilia was sentenced to death by suffocation in her own bath-house, but the flames failed to harm her. An executioner was sent to behead her, struck three times but failed to sever her head. She lingered for three days, distributing her goods to the poor and asking Pope Urban to convert her home into a church.

## 3. The Discovery of Her Relics

In **1599**, during the restoration of the Church of St. Cecilia in Trastevere (Rome), Cardinal Paolo Emilio Sfondrati ordered the opening of her tomb. Her body was found **incorrupt**, lying on her side with her head partially severed, her arms extended as if in prayer. The sculptor **Stefano Maderno** made a sketch of the body and later carved a life-size marble statue of Cecilia exactly as she was found, which now rests beneath the high altar of the church.

---

## 4. Patronage of Music

The first known association of St. Cecilia with music dates from the **15th century**. The tradition that she sang to God at her wedding feast ("while the musicians played, Cecilia sang in her heart to the Lord") led to her becoming the **patron saint of musicians**.

Numerous musical societies, conservatories, and choirs are named after her. The **Academy of St. Cecilia** in Rome is one of the oldest musical institutions in the world. Composers throughout history have honoured her with works, including Purcell, Handel, Gounod, and Britten.

## 7. Iconography

St. Cecilia is traditionally depicted with:

- **A small organ or other musical instrument** (her patronage of music)
- **A crown of roses and lilies** (the angelic crowns)
- **A sword** (her martyrdom)
- **A book of music** (sacred song)
