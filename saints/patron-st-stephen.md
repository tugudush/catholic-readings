# St. Stephen: Protomartyr

> **Note:** This document was composed with the assistance of Magisterium AI. Citations should be verified against primary sources where possible.

---

## 1. Introduction

**St. Stephen** (Greek: ***Στέφανος***, *Stephanos*, "crown"; Hebrew: ***סטיבן***; c. 5 – c. 34 A.D.) is honoured as the **first Christian martyr** (protomartyr). He was one of the **first seven deacons** appointed by the Apostles to serve the early Christian community in Jerusalem. His preaching, his vision of Christ at the right hand of God, and his prayer for his executioners at the moment of his stoning have inspired the Church for two millennia. His feast day is **26 December** (the day after Christmas).

> "Look, I see the heavens opened and the Son of Man standing at the right hand of God!"
> — St. Stephen (Acts 7:56)

---

## 2. The Appointment as Deacon

### The First Deacons

As the Church in Jerusalem grew, the Apostles appointed **seven men of good reputation, full of the Spirit and of wisdom** to serve the needs of the community, especially the distribution of food to widows (Acts 6:1–6). Stephen is named **first** among them. This event is traditionally considered the institution of the **diaconate** .

### Full of Grace and Power

Luke records that Stephen was "full of grace and power" and "did great wonders and signs among the people" (Acts 6:8).

---

## 3. The Trial Before the Sanhedrin

### The Disputation

Stephen's preaching provoked opposition from members of the Synagogue of the Freedmen, who could not withstand the wisdom and Spirit with which he spoke. They **falsely accused** him of blasphemy against Moses and God, and he was brought before the **Sanhedrin** (Acts 6:9–14).

### The Speech (Acts 7)

Stephen's defence before the Sanhedrin is the longest speech in the Acts of the Apostles. He recounted the history of Israel from **Abraham** through **Moses** to **Solomon**, demonstrating that God's presence is not limited to the Temple and that the Jewish leaders had consistently resisted the Holy Spirit, just as their ancestors had rejected the prophets.

---

## 4. The Martyrdom

### The Vision

As Stephen concluded his speech, he **gazed into heaven** and declared: "Look, I see the heavens opened and the Son of Man standing at the right hand of God!" (Acts 7:56).

### The Stoning

The Sanhedrin, enraged, **dragged him out of the city and stoned him**. His executioners laid their coats at the feet of a young man named **Saul of Tarsus**—the future **St. Paul the Apostle** (Acts 7:58).

### The Prayer for His Persecutors

As the stones struck him, Stephen **knelt down** and cried out:

> "Lord Jesus, receive my spirit."
> And falling to his knees, he cried out with a loud voice: **"Lord, do not hold this sin against them."**
> — Acts 7:59–60

This prayer, echoing Christ's own prayer from the Cross ("Father, forgive them"), has become a model of Christian forgiveness.

## 7. The Relics

According to tradition, the relics of St. Stephen were discovered in **415 A.D.** near Jerusalem. They were translated to Constantinople and later to Rome, where they rest in the **Basilica of St. Lawrence Outside the Walls**.
