# St. John the Baptist: The Forerunner of the Lord

> **Note:** This document was composed with the assistance of Magisterium AI. Citations should be verified against primary sources where possible.

---

## 1. Introduction

**St. John the Baptist** (Hebrew: ***יוֹחָנָן הַמַּטְבִּיל***, *Yoḥanan ha-Matbil*; Greek: ***Ἰωάννης ὁ Βαπτιστής***; Latin: *Ioannes Baptista*; c. 5 B.C. – c. 30 A.D.) is the **last and greatest of the Old Testament prophets** and the **Forerunner of the Messiah**. He prepared the way for the public ministry of Jesus Christ by preaching a baptism of repentance for the forgiveness of sins. He is honoured as a saint in all Christian traditions and is also revered in Islam as a prophet. His principal feast days are **24 June** (Nativity) and **29 August** (Beheading).

> "I baptize you with water; but one who is more powerful than I is coming; I am not worthy to untie the thong of his sandals."
> — St. John the Baptist (Luke 3:16)

---

## 2. Biblical Portrait

### Birth Announced by Gabriel

John's birth was foretold by the **Archangel Gabriel** to his father, **Zechariah**, a priest of the course of Abijah, who was serving in the Temple (Luke 1:5–25). Zechariah was struck mute for his disbelief until the child's birth.

The angel declared that John would be "great in the sight of the Lord" and "filled with the Holy Spirit even before his birth," and that he would "turn many of the children of Israel to the Lord their God" (Luke 1:15–16).

### The Visitation

When the pregnant Mary visited her cousin **Elizabeth** (John's mother), the infant John **leaped in her womb** at the presence of the unborn Jesus (Luke 1:39–45)—the first recognition of the Messiah by a human being.

### Birth and Circumcision

John was born to Zechariah and Elizabeth in their old age, a miracle of God's power. At his circumcision on the eighth day, Zechariah, his tongue loosed, prophesied the **Benedictus**:

> "And you, child, will be called the prophet of the Most High, for you will go before the Lord to prepare his ways."
> — Luke 1:76

## 3. Life in the Wilderness

John spent his early years in **the wilderness** of Judea, living a life of austerity and asceticism. He wore a garment of camel's hair with a leather belt and ate locusts and wild honey (Matthew 3:4)—the traditional garb and diet of a prophet, directly reminiscent of **Elijah**.

---

## 4. The Baptism of Repentance

### The Voice in the Wilderness

Around **28–29 A.D.**, John began his public ministry at the **River Jordan**, preaching: "Repent, for the kingdom of heaven has come near!" (Matthew 3:2). He baptized those who confessed their sins, with a baptism of repentance that foreshadowed the Christian sacrament.

He fulfilled the prophecy of **Isaiah 40:3**:

> "The voice of one crying out in the wilderness: 'Prepare the way of the Lord, make his paths straight.'"

### The Sermons of John

John's preaching was direct and challenging. He called the Pharisees and Sadducees a "brood of vipers" and insisted that repentance must bear fruit: "Bear fruits worthy of repentance" (Matthew 3:8). He gave practical moral instruction to tax collectors, soldiers, and the general populace (Luke 3:10–14).

### The Baptism of Jesus

Jesus Himself came to the Jordan to be baptized by John. John initially hesitated, declaring: "I need to be baptized by you, and do you come to me?" Jesus insisted, "to fulfill all righteousness" (Matthew 3:13–15). At Jesus's baptism, the Holy Spirit descended like a dove, and the Father's voice was heard: "This is my Son, the Beloved, with whom I am well pleased" (Matthew 3:17).

After this, John gave his definitive testimony: **"Behold, the Lamb of God, who takes away the sin of the world!"** (John 1:29).

## 5. Witness to Christ

### "He Must Increase, I Must Decrease"

John's disciples came to him concerned that Jesus was also baptizing and attracting followers. John responded with one of the most beautiful statements of humility in Scripture:

> "No one can receive anything except what is given them from heaven. You yourselves are my witnesses that I said, 'I am not the Messiah, but I have been sent ahead of him.' He who has the bride is the bridegroom. The friend of the bridegroom, who stands and hears him, rejoices greatly at the bridegroom's voice. For this reason my joy has been fulfilled. **He must increase, but I must decrease.** "
> — John 3:27–30

### The Testimony to Jesus's Divinity

John testified that he saw the Spirit descend upon Jesus and declared: "I myself have seen and have testified that this is the Son of God" (John 1:34).

## 6. Imprisonment and Martyrdom

### Rebuke of Herod Antipas

John fearlessly rebuked **Herod Antipas**, the tetrarch of Galilee, for marrying **Herodias**, his brother Philip's wife, in violation of Jewish law (Mark 6:18). Herod imprisoned John in the fortress of **Machaerus** on the Dead Sea.

### The Beheading

At a birthday banquet for Herod, the daughter of Herodias (traditionally named **Salome**) danced before the king and so pleased him that he promised to give her whatever she asked. Instigated by her mother, the girl demanded: "Give me the head of John the Baptist on a platter" (Mark 6:25).

Herod, though grieved, complied for the sake of his oath. John was **beheaded in prison**, and his head was brought on a platter to the girl, who gave it to her mother. His disciples came and buried his body (Mark 6:21–29).

### The First to Die for Christ

John is the **last of the Old Testament prophets** and the **first witness of the New Testament to die for Christ**, thus bridging the two covenants in his life and death.

## 9. The Baptism of Christ in Iconography

John is traditionally depicted as an ascetic figure wearing camel skin, holding a staff shaped like a cross (a *crux hastata* or processional cross), and pointing toward Christ. In Eastern iconography, he is shown with wings, as the "angel of the desert" foretold by Malachi (Malachi 3:1).
