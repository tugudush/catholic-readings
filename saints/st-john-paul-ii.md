# St. John Paul II: Pope of the Family and the Great Jubilee

## 1. Introduction

**St. John Paul II** (Latin: *Ioannes Paulus II*; Polish: *Jan Paweł II*; 18 May 1920 – 2 April 2005), born **Karol Józef Wojtyła**, served as the **264th Pope** of the Catholic Church from **16 October 1978** until his death in 2005. He was the first non-Italian pope in 455 years (since Pope Adrian VI, 1522–1523) and the first Polish pope in history. His pontificate of **26 years, 5 months, and 17 days** is one of the longest in history. He was canonized on **27 April 2014**. His feast day is **22 October**.

> "Do not be afraid. Open wide the doors for Christ!"
> — Pope John Paul II, Inaugural Homily, 22 October 1978

---

## 2. Early Life

### Birth and Family

Karol Józef Wojtyła was born on **18 May 1920** in **Wadowice, Poland**, about 50 kilometres southwest of Kraków. He was the youngest of three children born to **Karol Wojtyła Sr.**, a non-commissioned officer in the Austro-Hungarian army, and **Emilia Kaczorowska**, a teacher. His mother died in 1929, his elder brother Edmund (a doctor) died in 1932, and his father died in 1941—leaving young Karol entirely alone by age twenty-one.

### Youth and Education

Young Karol excelled in academics and athletics. He was an accomplished actor and playwright in his youth, deeply involved in the **Rhapsodic Theatre** movement, which preserved Polish culture through underground performances during the Nazi occupation.

### The Call to Priesthood

During the **Nazi occupation of Poland** (1939–1945), Karol worked as a manual labourer in a quarry (1940–1941) and later in a chemical factory (Solvay) to avoid deportation. In **1942**, he felt called to the priesthood and began **clandestine studies** in the underground seminary of Kraków, run by Cardinal Adam Sapieha.

---

## 3. Priest and Bishop

### Ordination

Karol Wojtyła was ordained a priest on **1 November 1946** by Cardinal Sapieha. He was sent to **Rome** for doctoral studies, earning a doctorate in theology in 1948 with a dissertation on **St. John of the Cross** and the nature of faith.

### Pastoral Work

After returning to Poland, Fr. Wojtyła served as a parish priest and university chaplain, where he became renowned for his pastoral work with young people. He taught ethics at the **Jagiellonian University** and the **Catholic University of Lublin**.

### Bishop and Archbishop

- **1958**: Appointed **Auxiliary Bishop of Kraków** by Pope Pius XII.
- **1964**: Appointed **Archbishop of Kraków** by Pope Paul VI.
- **1967**: Created **Cardinal** by Pope Paul VI.

He participated in all four sessions of the **Second Vatican Council** (1962–1965), contributing significantly to the **Pastoral Constitution *Gaudium et Spes*** and the **Declaration on Religious Freedom *Dignitatis Humanae***.

## 4. The Papacy

### Election

On **16 October 1978**, after the sudden death of Pope John Paul I (who served only 33 days), Cardinal Wojtyła was elected pope on the eighth ballot. He took the name **John Paul II** in honour of his predecessor.

### The First Pilgrim Pope

John Paul II was the **most-travelled pope in history**, visiting **129 countries** over 104 foreign apostolic journeys. He brought the papacy to the world in an unprecedented way, earning the title "the Pilgrim Pope."

### Key Accomplishments

| Area | Accomplishments |
|------|----------------|
| **Encyclicals** | 14 encyclicals, including *Redemptor Hominis* (1979), *Dives in Misericordia* (1980), *Evangelium Vitae* (1995), *Fides et Ratio* (1998), *Ecclesia de Eucharistia* (2003) |
| **Theology of the Body** | A series of 129 catecheses (1979–1984) on human love, marriage, and sexuality, now known as the **Theology of the Body** |
| **Canonizations** | 482 saints and 1,338 beatifications—more than all predecessors combined |
| **World Youth Day** | Founded in 1985; drew millions of young Catholics to faith |
| **Catechism of the Catholic Church** | Promulgated in 1992, the first universal catechism since the Council of Trent |
| **Interfaith Dialogue** | The **Assisi Peace Meetings** (1986, 1993, 2002); historic visit to the Rome Synagogue (1986); first papal visit to a mosque (Damascus, 2001) |
| **Fall of Communism** | His 1979 pilgrimage to Poland is credited with sparking the Solidarity movement that led to the peaceful fall of communism in Eastern Europe |

### The Assassination Attempt

On **13 May 1981**, the Feast of Our Lady of Fatima, John Paul II was **shot and gravely wounded** in St. Peter's Square by Mehmet Ali Ağca. He publicly forgave his would-be assassin and credited Our Lady of Fatima with saving his life.

## 5. Suffering and Death

### Parkinson's Disease

In his final years, John Paul II visibly suffered from **Parkinson's disease**, yet he continued his public ministry with extraordinary fortitude, transforming his suffering into a witness to the dignity of human life in all its stages.

### Death

He died on **2 April 2005**, the vigil of **Divine Mercy Sunday**, at the age of 84. His final words were: **"Let me go to the house of the Father."**

His funeral on **8 April 2005** was one of the largest gatherings in human history, with millions of pilgrims in Rome and billions watching worldwide.

## 6. Canonization

- **Beatified**: **1 May 2011** by Pope Benedict XVI (Divine Mercy Sunday).
- **Canonized**: **27 April 2014** (Divine Mercy Sunday) by Pope **Francis**.
- **Feast Day**: **22 October**, the anniversary of his inaugural Mass.
