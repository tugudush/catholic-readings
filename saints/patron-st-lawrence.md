# St. Lawrence: Deacon and Protomartyr of Rome

## 1. Introduction

**St. Lawrence** (Latin: *Laurentius*; Spanish: *San Lorenzo*; c. 225 – 10 August 258 A.D.) was one of the **seven deacons** of the Church of Rome under **Pope St. Sixtus II** and is one of the most venerated martyrs of the early Church. He is the **patron saint of cooks, librarians, and the poor**. His martyrdom by gridiron—famously joking with his executioners—has made him an enduring symbol of faith and humour in the face of death. His feast day is **10 August**.

> "I am roasted on one side; if you would have me well cooked, turn me over!"
> — St. Lawrence to his executioners

---

## 2. Life and Ministry

### Archdeacon of Rome

Lawrence was one of the **seven deacons** of the Church of Rome, serving **Pope St. Sixtus II** (257–258). As the **archdeacon**, he was responsible for the Church's treasury, its sacred vessels, and the distribution of alms to the poor.

### The Treasurer of the Church

In **258 A.D.**, the Emperor **Valerian** decreed that all bishops, priests, and deacons must sacrifice to the Roman gods or face immediate execution. On **6 August 258**, Pope Sixtus II was arrested and **beheaded** while celebrating the liturgy in the catacombs.

## 3. The Encounter with the Prefect

### The Demand for the Church's Treasures

According to the account of St. Ambrose of Milan and St. Augustine of Hippo, the Roman **Prefect** demanded that Lawrence hand over the Church's treasures. Lawrence asked for three days to gather them. Instead of collecting gold and silver, Lawrence gathered the **poor, the sick, the widows, and the orphans** of Rome—the "treasures of the Church"—and presented them to the Prefect.

> "These are the treasures of the Church. They never diminish, but rather increase. They are distributed to all, yet none is left without his portion."

### The Martyrdom

The Prefect, enraged, ordered Lawrence to be **martyred on a gridiron** (a metal grate) over a slow fire. Lawrence's courage and good humour during his prolonged execution became legendary. As the fire slowly roasted his body, Lawrence is said to have called out to his executioners: **"Turn me over; I am done on this side!"**

## 6. Veneration

The **Basilica of St. Lawrence Outside the Walls** in Rome (San Lorenzo fuori le Mura) is one of the seven pilgrimage churches of Rome and houses his tomb.

The **Escorial** palace-monastery in Spain is dedicated to St. Lawrence, built in the shape of a gridiron to honour his martyrdom.
