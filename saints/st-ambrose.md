# St. Ambrose of Milan: Father and Doctor of the Church

> **Note:** This document was composed with the assistance of Magisterium AI. Citations should be verified against primary sources where possible.

---

## 1. Introduction

**St. Ambrose of Milan** (Latin: *Ambrosius Mediolanensis*; Italian: *Ambrogio di Milano*; c. 339 – 4 April 397 A.D.) was a Roman politician who, while serving as the imperial governor of Aemilia-Liguria, was unexpectedly acclaimed **Bishop of Milan** by popular acclamation—even though he was not yet baptized. He became one of the four **Great Doctors of the Latin Church** (along with Augustine, Jerome, and Gregory the Great), a fierce defender of Nicene orthodoxy against Arianism, and the mentor and baptizer of **St. Augustine of Hippo**. His feast day is **7 December**.

> "If you are in Rome, live in the Roman way; if you are elsewhere, live as they do. Avoid giving scandal to anyone."
> — St. Ambrose of Milan

---

## 2. Early Life

### Birth and Family

Ambrose was born around **339 A.D.** in **Trier**, Gaul (modern-day Germany), to a Roman Christian family of the senatorial class. His father, also named Ambrosius, was the **Praetorian Prefect of Gaul**—one of the highest imperial offices. After his father's death, the family moved to **Rome**, where Ambrose received a classical education in law, rhetoric, and literature.

### Legal Career

Ambrose pursued a legal career in Rome and was appointed **Governor of Aemilia and Liguria** (northern Italy) with his seat in **Milan**, the imperial capital of the Western Roman Empire. He was an effective and respected administrator.

---

## 3. The Election as Bishop

### The Arian Crisis

In **374 A.D.**, the Arian Bishop of Milan, **Auxentius**, died. The city was deeply divided between Nicene Catholics and Arians. Ambrose, as governor, went to the cathedral to maintain order during the election.

### The Acclamation

As Ambrose addressed the crowd, a child's voice reportedly cried out: **"Ambrose for bishop!"** The entire assembly took up the cry. Ambrose was utterly surprised—he was not yet baptized, and he protested vigorously, trying to escape by night. He was eventually persuaded, baptized, and consecrated **Bishop of Milan on 7 December 374**.

---

## 4. The Bishop of Milan

### Theological Formation

Ambrose immediately applied himself to theology. He was ordained a priest and began to study Scripture and the Fathers under **Simplician**, a renowned theologian. Within a week of his baptism, he was a bishop.

### Defender of Nicene Orthodoxy

Ambrose was a tireless defender of Nicene orthodoxy against **Arianism**, which had powerful support at the imperial court. He refused to surrender church buildings to Arian bishops, leading a famous **standoff at the Basilica of Portiana** with the Empress **Justina** in 385–386. When imperial troops surrounded the basilica, Ambrose and his congregation locked themselves inside and sang hymns composed by Ambrose himself—the beginning of **Ambrosian hymnody** in the Western Church.

### The Penance of Emperor Theodosius

In **390 A.D.**, after the Emperor **Theodosius I** ordered a massacre of 7,000 citizens in Thessalonica following a riot, Ambrose **excommunicated the emperor** and refused him entry to the church until he had done public penance. Theodosius submitted, doing penance in his imperial robes—a landmark moment in the Church's assertion of moral authority over the state.

---

## 5. Mentor of St. Augustine

Ambrose's preaching in Milan was instrumental in the conversion of **St. Augustine of Hippo**. Augustine, then a skeptical Manichaean professor of rhetoric, was drawn by Ambrose's eloquence and depth. Ambrose baptized Augustine at the Easter Vigil of 387, along with Augustine's son Adeodatus and his friend Alypius.

---

## 6. Writings

### Hymns

Ambrose is considered the **father of Latin hymnody**. He composed many hymns that are still sung, including:

- *Veni Redemptor Gentium* ("Come, Redeemer of the Nations")
- *Deus Creator Omnium* ("God, Creator of All")
- *Aeterne Rerum Conditor* ("Eternal Founder of the World")

### Theological Works

- ***De Officiis Ministrorum*** (On the Duties of the Clergy): A manual of moral theology for clergy.
- ***De Fide*** (On the Faith): A defence of Nicene orthodoxy.
- ***De Spiritu Sancto*** (On the Holy Spirit): A defence of the divinity of the Holy Spirit.
- ***De Sacramentis*** (On the Sacraments): A catechetical work on baptism, confirmation, and the Eucharist.
- ***De Paenitentia*** (On Penance): On the sacrament of reconciliation.

---

## 7. Death

Ambrose died on **4 April 397** (Holy Saturday) at the age of about fifty-seven. He was buried in the Basilica of the Holy Martyrs (later the **Basilica of St. Ambrose**) in Milan.
