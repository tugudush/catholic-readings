# St. Gregory the Great: Pope and Doctor of the Church

> **Note:** This document was composed with the assistance of Magisterium AI. Citations should be verified against primary sources where possible.

---

## 1. Introduction

**St. Gregory the Great** (Latin: *Gregorius Magnus*; c. 540 – 12 March 604 A.D.) was **Pope from 590 to 604** and one of the four **Great Doctors of the Latin Church** (with Augustine, Ambrose, and Jerome). He is known for his reform of the liturgy, his missionary initiatives (including the sending of St. Augustine of Canterbury to England), his prolific writings, and his development of **Gregorian chant**. His feast day is **3 September** (formerly 12 March).

> "The Scriptures are a river in which the lamb may wade and the elephant may swim."
> — St. Gregory the Great

---

## 2. Early Life

### Birth and Family

Gregory was born around **540 A.D.** in **Rome**, to an aristocratic Roman family with a strong tradition of faith. His father, **Gordianus**, was a senator and a regionarius of the Church; his mother, **Silvia**, is also venerated as a saint. His family owned extensive estates in Sicily and a mansion on the Caelian Hill (now the Church of San Gregorio Magno).

### Education and Career

Gregory received an excellent education in law, rhetoric, and literature. He followed his father's path into civil service and, by his early thirties, had become **Prefect of Rome**—the highest civil office in the city.

---

## 3. The Call to Monastic Life

### The Conversion

After his father's death, Gregory converted his family mansion into a **Benedictine monastery** dedicated to St. Andrew. He himself became a monk, embracing a life of poverty, prayer, and study. He founded six other monasteries on his Sicilian estates.

### The Papal Representative

Pope Benedict I ordained Gregory as a **deacon** and sent him as his representative (*apocrisiarius*) to the imperial court in **Constantinople**, where he served for about six years.

---

## 4. Pope Gregory the Great

### Election as Pope

After the death of Pope Pelagius II during a plague, Gregory was **unanimously elected Pope** in **590 A.D.** He initially tried to refuse, even fleeing the city, but was forced to accept the office.

### The Gregorian Mission to England

One of Gregory's most famous initiatives was sending **St. Augustine of Canterbury** and about forty monks to **evangelize the Anglo-Saxons of England** in **596 A.D.** According to tradition, Gregory had been inspired to this mission years earlier when he saw fair-haired Anglo-Saxon slave boys in the Roman Forum and was struck by their beauty, remarking that they were "not Angles but Angels."

### Reform of the Liturgy

Gregory is credited with major reforms of the Roman liturgy, including:

- The organization and systematization of the **Roman Mass**.
- The development and codification of **Gregorian chant** (named after him, though much of the musical tradition predates him).
- The addition of the **Kyrie eleison**, the **Gloria**, and the **Pater Noster** to the Mass.

### The Pastoral Care of the Church

Gregory wrote ***Regula Pastoralis*** (*Pastoral Rule*), a handbook for bishops on the care of souls, which became a standard text throughout the Middle Ages. He also reorganized the administration of the Church's vast estates (the Patrimony of St. Peter) to feed the poor and ransom captives.

## 5. Major Works

| Work | Content |
|------|---------|
| ***Regula Pastoralis*** (Pastoral Rule) | A manual on the duties of a bishop and the care of souls |
| ***Moralia in Job*** | A comprehensive commentary on the Book of Job |
| ***Homilies on the Gospels*** | Forty homilies on the Gospels for the liturgical year |
| ***Homilies on Ezechiel*** | Homilies on the prophet Ezekiel |
| ***Dialogues*** | Four books on the lives and miracles of Italian saints, including St. Benedict |

---

## 6. Death

Gregory died on **12 March 604** after a long and painful illness. He was buried in **St. Peter's Basilica** in Rome. He was immediately venerated as a saint.

## 7. Patronage

St. Gregory the Great is the patron saint of:

| Patronage | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Popes and Church Leaders** | As a model pope |
| **Musicians and Chanters** | His development of Gregorian chant |
| **Teachers and Students** | His prolific writings |
| **England** | As the architect of the English mission |
| **Against Plague** | His intercession during the plague of 590 |
