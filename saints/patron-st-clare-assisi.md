# St. Clare of Assisi: Foundress of the Poor Clares

> **Note:** This document was composed with the assistance of Magisterium AI. Citations should be verified against primary sources where possible.

---

## 1. Introduction

**St. Clare of Assisi** (Italian: *Chiara d'Offreducci*; Latin: *Clara Assisiensis*; 16 July 1194 – 11 August 1253) was an Italian Catholic nun and the **foundress of the Order of Poor Ladies** (the **Poor Clares**), the second order of the Franciscan movement. She was the first woman to write a monastic rule for women and is honoured as the **patron saint of television** due to a vision in which she "saw" the celebration of Christmas Mass from her convent. Her feast day is **11 August**.

> "Go forth without fear, for He who created you has made you holy, has always protected you, and loves you as a mother."
> — St. Clare of Assisi, *Letter to Blessed Agnes of Prague*

---

## 2. Early Life

### Birth and Family

Clare was born on **16 July 1194** in **Assisi, Italy**, the eldest daughter of **Favarone Offreduccio di Scifi**, a wealthy knight, and **Ortolana**, a noblewoman known for her piety and pilgrimage to the Holy Land. Clare's family was among the nobility of Assisi.

### Early Piety

From childhood, Clare was known for her prayerfulness, her love of the poor, and her desire to live a life of holiness. She was influenced by the preaching and example of **St. Francis of Assisi**, who was already drawing crowds with his message of Gospel poverty.

---

## 3. The Flight to Francis

### Palm Sunday 1212

On **Palm Sunday, 18 March 1212**, Clare attended Mass at the Cathedral of Assisi. While the other young women came forward to receive the blessed palms, Clare hesitated. The **Bishop of Assisi** came down from the altar and placed the palm in her hands—a sign of what was to come.

### The Night of Departure

On the night of **Palm Sunday**, Clare left her family home through a secret door (a "postern gate") and fled to the **Portiuncula**, the little chapel where Francis and his brothers were assembled. Francis received her, and in a simple ceremony, cut her hair and clothed her in a rough woollen habit.

---

## 4. The Order of Poor Ladies

### The First Convent

Clare was initially placed in a Benedictine convent for safety, but her family attempted to bring her back by force. She clung to the altar, uncovering her shorn head, and declared herself the spouse of Christ. Eventually, Francis settled her and her sister **Agnes** at the Church of **San Damiano** in Assisi, which he had rebuilt years earlier.

### The Life at San Damiano

Clare and her sisters lived in **absolute poverty**, depending entirely on alms. They owned nothing—not even the convent—and supported themselves through manual labour. Their life was one of contemplative prayer, silence, and austerity. Clare governed the community as **abbess** from **1215** until her death in 1253.

### The Rule of St. Clare

Clare's greatest achievement was her **Rule**, which she wrote herself—the first monastic rule composed by a woman. It received papal approval from **Pope Innocent IV** on **9 August 1253**, just two days before her death. The Rule committed the sisters to absolute poverty, forbidding the convent from holding any property or fixed income.

## 5. Miracles and Spiritual Gifts

### The Miracle of the Eucharist

In **1240**, an invading army of Saracens (hired by Emperor Frederick II) attacked Assisi and climbed the walls of San Damiano. Clare, who was ill and bedridden, had the **Blessed Sacrament** brought in a pyx and held it up before the invaders. According to the account, a brilliant light shone from the Eucharist, and the attackers fled.

### The Miracle of the Multiplication of Oil

When the convent ran out of oil for cooking, Clare blessed the empty vessel, and it was miraculously filled.

### The Christmas Vision

In **1252**, Clare was too ill to attend the Christmas midnight Mass at the Basilica of St. Francis. In a vision, she saw and heard the entire Mass on the wall of her cell. For this reason, **Pope Pius XII** proclaimed her **patron saint of television** in **1958**.

---

## 6. Death and Canonization

### Death

Clare died on **11 August 1253** at the age of fifty-nine, surrounded by her sisters. Her last words were a quiet prayer: "Blessed are You, O God, who have created me."

### Canonization

- **Canonized**: **26 September 1255** by Pope **Alexander IV** (just two years after her death).
- **Feast Day**: **11 August**.

## 9. Iconography

St. Clare is traditionally depicted as a **Franciscan nun** holding:

- **A monstrance or ciborium** (the Eucharistic miracle)
- **A lily** (purity)
- **The Franciscan cord** with three knots
- **A lantern or lamp** (her Christmas vision)
- **A book** (her Rule)
