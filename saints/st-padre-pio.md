# St. Pio of Pietrelcina (Padre Pio): The Stigmatist of San Giovanni Rotondo

> **Note:** This document was composed with the assistance of Magisterium AI. Citations should be verified against primary sources where possible.

---

## 1. Introduction

**St. Pio of Pietrelcina** (Italian: *Pio da Pietrelcina*; 25 May 1887 – 23 September 1968), commonly known as **Padre Pio**, was an Italian Capuchin Franciscan friar, priest, and mystic who bore the **stigmata**—the visible wounds of Christ—for fifty years. He is one of the most popular saints of the modern era, venerated for his extraordinary spiritual gifts, his devotion to the Eucharist and confession, and his charitable works. His feast day is **23 September**.

> "Pray, hope, and don't worry. Worry is useless. God is merciful and will hear your prayer."
> — St. Pio of Pietrelcina

---

## 2. Early Life

### Birth and Family

Francesco Forgione was born on **25 May 1887** in **Pietrelcina**, a small agricultural town in the province of Benevento, southern Italy. He was the eldest of five children born to **Grazio Forgione**, a peasant farmer, and **Maria Giuseppa De Nunzio**. His parents were poor but devout, and Francesco was drawn to religious life from an early age.

### Early Spiritual Experiences

From childhood, Francesco experienced visions, ecstasies, and attacks by the devil. At age seven, he reportedly saw the **Blessed Virgin** and the **Child Jesus** in a vision. He served as an altar boy and was known for his intense prayer life and love of the Eucharist.

---

## 3. Religious Life

### Entry into the Capuchins

On **6 January 1903**, at age sixteen, Francesco entered the novitiate of the **Order of Friars Minor Capuchin** at **Morcone**. He took the name **Brother Pio** in honour of Pope St. Pius I. He made his solemn profession on **27 January 1907**.

### Ordination

Padre Pio was ordained a priest on **10 August 1910** in the Cathedral of Benevento. Due to his fragile health, he remained in his family home in Pietrelcina for several years, continuing his studies and spiritual life.

### San Giovanni Rotondo

In **1916**, Padre Pio was assigned to the remote convent of **San Giovanni Rotondo** in the region of Apulia, where he would spend the remaining fifty-two years of his life.

---

## 4. The Stigmata

### The Reception of the Wounds

On **20 September 1918**, while praying before a crucifix in the choir loft of the convent church, Padre Pio received the **visible stigmata**—the five wounds of Christ appeared on his hands, feet, and side. The wounds bled continuously and were accompanied by intense physical pain. They remained visible and unhealed for the next **fifty years**.

### Medical Investigation

The stigmata were examined by numerous doctors, some of whom were skeptical or hostile. Despite extensive medical scrutiny, no natural explanation was ever found. The wounds bled fresh blood daily, resisted all treatments, and emitted a fragrance described as "the odour of sanctity."

### The Church's Response

The Holy See initially subjected Padre Pio to restrictions, including a ban on saying Mass publicly and hearing confessions (1931–1933). After investigation, Pope Pius XI lifted the restrictions, and Padre Pio's ministry resumed with ecclesiastical approval.

---

## 5. Spiritual Gifts

In addition to the stigmata, Padre Pio was reported to possess numerous extraordinary spiritual gifts:

- **Bilocation**: Appearing in two places simultaneously.
- **Reading of Hearts**: Knowing the sins of penitents before they confessed them.
- **Healing Miracles**: Numerous testimonies of physical healings through his intercession.
- **Prophetic Visions**: Including the foreknowledge of future events.
- **The Odour of Sanctity**: A fragrant scent emanating from his body and wounds.

---

## 6. Ministry

### Confessor and Spiritual Director

Padre Pio's primary ministry was the **confessional**. He spent up to sixteen hours a day hearing confessions, drawing pilgrims from around the world. He had the reputation of being able to read the souls of penitents, sometimes revealing hidden sins or providing precise spiritual guidance.

### The Casa Sollievo della Sofferenza

In **1956**, Padre Pio founded the **"Casa Sollievo della Sofferenza"** ("House for the Relief of Suffering"), a modern hospital in San Giovanni Rotondo, to provide cutting-edge medical care to the poor. It remains one of the largest and most respected hospitals in southern Italy.

### Prayer Groups

Padre Pio encouraged the formation of **Prayer Groups**, which spread throughout the world and continue to thrive today.

---

## 7. Death

Padre Pio died on **23 September 1968** at the age of eighty-one. His final words were: **"Gesù, Maria"** ("Jesus, Mary"). His stigmata had completely disappeared a few hours before his death, leaving only smooth, healed skin with no scars.

### Canonization

- **Declared Venerable**: 18 December 1997 by Pope John Paul II.
- **Beatified**: **2 May 1999** by Pope John Paul II.
- **Canonized**: **16 June 2002** by Pope John Paul II.
- **Feast Day**: **23 September**.
