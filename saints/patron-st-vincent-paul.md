# St. Vincent de Paul: Apostle of Charity

> **Note:** This document was composed with the assistance of Magisterium AI. Citations should be verified against primary sources where possible.

## 1. Introduction

**St. Vincent de Paul** (French: *Vincent de Paul*; Latin: *Vincentius a Paulo*; 24 April 1581 – 27 September 1660) was a French Catholic priest and the **founder of the Congregation of the Mission** (the Vincentians or Lazarists) and the **Daughters of Charity**. He is renowned for his extraordinary work among the poor, his reform of the clergy, and his foundational role in the development of organized Catholic charity. His feast day is **27 September**.

> "Go to the poor: you will find God."
> — St. Vincent de Paul

---

## 2. Early Life

### Birth and Family

Vincent was born on **24 April 1581** in the village of **Pouy** (now Saint-Vincent-de-Paul) in the Landes region of southwestern France. He was the third of six children born to **Jean de Paul**, a poor peasant farmer, and **Bertrande de Moras**.

### Education

Vincent's intellectual gifts were recognized early, and his father sold the family oxen to pay for his education. He studied with the **Franciscans** in Dax and later at the **University of Toulouse**, where he earned a degree in theology. He was **ordained a priest on 23 September 1600** at the age of nineteen.

---

## 3. The Years of Formation

### Capture by Pirates

In **1605**, Vincent traveled by ship from Toulouse to Narbonne but was captured by **Barbary pirates** and taken as a slave to **Tunis**. He spent two years in slavery, sold from master to master. His last master, an apostate Christian, was converted by Vincent's patience and preaching, and both escaped to France in 1607.

### Spiritual Director to the Gondi Family

After his return, Vincent became the **chaplain and spiritual director** to the powerful **Gondi family** (Philippe-Emmanuel de Gondi, General of the Galleys, and his wife Françoise Marguerite de Silly). This position gave him access to the highest levels of French society.

### The Call to Serve the Poor

While serving the Gondi family, Vincent was struck by the spiritual and material poverty of the peasants on their estates. He began to organize missions to bring the Gospel to the rural poor.

---

## 4. The Congregation of the Mission (Vincentians)

### Foundation

In **1625**, Vincent founded the **Congregation of the Mission**, a community of priests and brothers dedicated to preaching missions among the poor, especially in rural areas. The community was approved by Pope Urban VIII in **1633**. They became known as **Lazarists** (from their priory of St. Lazare in Paris) or **Vincentians**.

### Missionary Work

The Vincentians preached parish missions, served the poor, and formed the clergy. They spread rapidly throughout France, Italy, Poland, and eventually to Africa, Madagascar, and the Americas.

---

## 5. The Daughters of Charity

### Co-foundation with St. Louise de Marillac

In **1633**, with **St. Louise de Marillac**, Vincent founded the **Daughters of Charity**, the first non-cloistered religious community of women dedicated to active works of mercy. Their rule was revolutionary: instead of staying in convents, they went out to serve the sick and poor in their homes and in hospitals.

### The Motto

The Daughters of Charity took as their motto: **"The charity of Christ urges us"** (*Caritas Christi urget nos*). Their habit was the blue-grey dress of a French peasant woman rather than a traditional religious habit—a sign of their identification with the poor.

---

## 6. Works of Charity

St. Vincent organized a vast network of charitable services:

### For the Poor
- **Confréries de la Charité** (Confraternities of Charity): Lay organizations of women who served the sick poor in their parishes.
- **Soup kitchens and distribution of food, clothing, and fuel**.

### For Foundlings
- Vincent established a home for **abandoned children**, which grew to care for thousands of foundlings.

### For Prisoners and Galley Slaves
- He served as **Chaplain General of the Galleys** and worked to improve the conditions of convicts and galley slaves.

### For the Sick
- The Daughters of Charity staffed the **Hôtel-Dieu** hospital in Paris and other hospitals.

### For the Clergy
- Vincent organized **Seminaries** for the training of priests and conducted **retreats for ordinands**.

---

## 7. Death and Canonization

### Death

Vincent died on **27 September 1660** at the age of seventy-nine, surrounded by his fellow Vincentians. He was buried at the Church of St. Lazare in Paris.

### Canonization

- **Beatified**: 13 August 1729 by Pope Benedict XIII.
- **Canonized**: **16 June 1737** by Pope Clement XII.
- **Patron of all charitable works**: Proclaimed by Pope Leo XIII in 1885.

---

## 8. The Society of St. Vincent de Paul

In **1833**, the **Society of St. Vincent de Paul** was founded in Paris by **Blessed Frédéric Ozanam** and a group of lay Catholics. This international lay organization is devoted to person-to-person service to the poor, following the spirit of St. Vincent. It is now present in over 150 countries.

## 11. The Vincentian Charism

The core of Vincent's spirituality is the conviction that **Christ is especially present in the poor**. His spiritual legacy includes:

- **Seeing Christ in the poor**: "You will find God in the poor."
- **Effective organization**: Charity must not be random but organized and sustainable.
- **Humility**: "The poor are our masters."
- **Collaboration**: Lay people and clergy working together in service.
